The Big Picture

What to expect
  • About 2 hours – one test, one sitting. No scheduled break; if you need one, raise your hand, but the clock keeps running.
  • Fixed order – Speaking & Writing first, then Reading, then Listening. You cannot skip or reorder sections.
  • 22 question types – you'll practice every one of them over the next three days. Today is just the map.

How You're Marked

AI + human review
  • AI scores every task – all 22 task types are scored by Pearson's AI system. The algorithm evaluates your responses against thousands of scored samples.
  • Results delivered in about 48 hours. Scores are valid for two years.
  • Human expert review – six tasks get a second look before your score is final:
    • Describe Image · Retell Lecture · Summarize Group Discussion
    • Respond to a Situation · Write Essay · Summarize Spoken Text
Source: pearsonpte.com/pte-academic/scoring

What's New Since 2025

Two new speaking tasks

The 2026 test adds two new speaking tasks. Both reward natural conversation — not memorised scripts.

Respond to a Situation

You'll hear a short scenario and respond as if you're in it. Natural, spontaneous speech — not rehearsed.

Live Warm‑Up

Feel the clock · No scoring
⏱️ 40s

Describe the Lounge

Stand in the lounge. Set your phone timer to 40 seconds.

Describe this room to a person who has never entered it. Cover the wall hangings, the seating, the floor, and the window light. Talk about the colours and the mood.

This is close to the real Describe Image task. No scoring, no pressure — just a feel for talking against a clock.

💡 Coach tip: If 40 seconds feels short, that's exactly the point. The real task feels the same way at first. You'll get used to it.
⏱️ 40s · with ambient noise

Describe the Kitchen

Stand in the kitchen. Play your chatter audio. Set your timer to 40 seconds.

Describe this kitchen to a person who has never seen it. Cover the counters, the stove, the sink, and the cupboards. Note the light, the tiles, and the overall feel.

This mimics the real test room with other candidates speaking nearby. No scoring, just volume and focus against the clock.

💡 Coach tip: If 40 seconds feels short, that's exactly the point. The real task feels the same way at first. You'll get used to it.
